This page shows the TF-IDF scores for words in each decade, which is a way of showing which words stand out each decade when referenced to the entire century. Words that have a high TF-IDF score in the 1610's, for example, occur a lot in pamphlets from the 1610's but not as often in the other decades.

A fairly interesting occurence in pamphlets from 1600 to 1610 is the word honinonero, which appears to be part of a chorus of a ballad. This ballad is also found in a collection named The Shirburn Ballads. These ballads date from 1585 to 1610, and were found in the library of the Earl of Macclesfield in Oxfordshire. It's not uncommon for songs and poems to be spread in pamphlets during this time. You can read the pamphlet that contains the curious word here.
