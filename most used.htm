Most used words in English pamphlets in the seventeenth century

In pamphlets all throughout the seventeenth century, the most used words seem to be very similar; they are all words pertaining to god or royalty. In the seventeenth century, religion and the church were deeply intertwined with daily life as well as politics. Interesting is that in the pamphlets from 1640 to 1650, the word 'parliament' appears to be the most prevalent word. This likely has to do with the English Civil War, which lasted from 1642 until 1651. The Civil War was made up of a series of upsets, where Parliamentarians fought against Royalists in order to establish the right of Parliament to participate in government. The Civil War marked a turning point in English history, challenging the idea of absolute monarchy.
